## Design Context

### Users
Primarily donors, adopters/fosters, volunteers, and overnight guests. Mix of Central Texas locals and national animal advocates, often arriving via social media or word of mouth. They already care about animals — the site's job is to show them SARA's scale and give them a clear path to act. Content editor (Tracy) manages news, events, and tributes through a CMS; she is non-technical.

### Brand Personality
**Compassionate, purposeful, calm.**

Voice is honest and direct — SARA doesn't soften hard truths ("no animal is ever killed for convenience"). Tone is warm without being sentimental, grounded in fact and place: 30 years, 128 acres of Central Texas prairie, 750 animals. It earns trust rather than manufacturing emotion.

### Aesthetic Direction
Organic and editorial. Warm earthy palette — forest green, cream, rust/terracotta, gold, sage — anchored by Fraunces (display serif) and Inter (body). Feels rooted in a real place, not produced in a marketing department.

**Anti-reference:** Corporate charity aesthetic — no polished stock photography of sad animals, no manipulative urgency patterns, none of the slick design language of large national nonprofits (ASPCA, HSUS style). This is a real place with real animals; the design should feel that way.

**Emotional arc for first-time visitors:** Awe first (scale and scope of SARA), then quiet inspiration (the mission and the animals), then a pull to act (donate, adopt, volunteer, visit). The sequence matters — don't lead with the ask.

### Design Principles

1. **Let the mission lead.** The animals and the numbers do the convincing. Design creates space and clarity, not hype. Resist decorative complexity.

2. **Earned trust over manufactured emotion.** Real photography, honest copy, specific facts (750 animals, 128 acres, $1,500/year per dog). Never manipulate; inform and invite.

3. **Calm confidence.** Generous whitespace, unhurried reading pace. Urgency when genuinely warranted, never ambient anxiety. The sanctuary is a safe place — the site should feel like one.

4. **Rooted in place.** Central Texas prairie, a red barn, dusk light, wide-open land. Design choices should feel located, not generic. The palette, photography, and copy should all reinforce where this is.

5. **Accessible and unambiguous.** WCAG AA minimum. Sufficient contrast, keyboard navigable, readable at any viewport. Clarity is itself a form of respect for the audience.
